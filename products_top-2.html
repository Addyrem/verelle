Materials in this product explained
Cotton is a natural fiber derived from the fluffy seed fibers of the cotton plant (genus Gossypium). Unlike synthetic fibers, cotton is not produced from crude oil; instead, it is a renewable and biodegradable resource. The cotton plant is cultivated for its soft and fluffy fibers, which are used to create a wide range of textiles and products. This natural fiber has been a staple in the textile industry for centuries, known for its breathability, comfort, and versatility. Embraced for its sustainable and eco-friendly qualities, cotton stands in contrast to synthetic fibers like polyester, making it a popular choice for those who prioritize natural and renewable materials.
